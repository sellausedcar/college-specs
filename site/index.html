    About application essays & prompts

    The Application essay row shows whether each school considers a personal statement. The actual prompts aren't a bundled dataset (they're copyrighted and rewritten each cycle), and no site lets you open one school's prompts by name — so each cell links you to a supplemental-prompt database where you look up the school: My Supplementals (1,159 schools, verified 2025–26) or CollegeVine (top-100 schools, 2024–25).

    💡 Tip: clicking a “see prompts” link copies that school's name to your clipboard — on the database, just click the search box and press Ctrl+V (⌘V on Mac) to fill it in. No typing needed.

    Most applicants also write the shared Common App personal statement — one of these seven prompts (2026–2027):

    1. Some students have a background, identity, interest, or talent that is so meaningful they believe their application would be incomplete without it. If this sounds like you, then please share your story.
    2. The lessons we take from obstacles we encounter can be fundamental to later success. Recount a time when you faced a challenge, setback, or failure. How did it affect you, and what did you learn from the experience?
    3. Reflect on a time when you questioned or challenged a belief or idea. What prompted your thinking? What was the outcome?
    4. Reflect on something that someone has done for you that has made you happy or thankful in a surprising way. How has this gratitude affected or motivated you?
    5. Discuss an accomplishment, event, or realization that sparked a period of personal growth and a new understanding of yourself or others.
    6. Describe a topic, idea, or concept you find so engaging that it makes you lose all track of time. Why does it captivate you? What or who do you turn to when you want to learn more?
    7. Share an essay on any topic of your choice. It can be one you've already written, one that responds to a different prompt, or one of your own design.

    Personal-statement prompts source: The Common Application (250–650 words). Prompts and supplements change yearly — always confirm on the school's own application.

    Sources: College Scorecard · IPEDS · Opportunity Insights · Campus Safety & Security (Clery Act)

    Clery figures are reported on-campus incidents — reporting practices vary and rates are not a complete measure of safety. SAT/ACT ranges are sparse where schools are test-optional. "N/A" usually means the statistic was suppressed for privacy or not reported. Not affiliated with any school or agency.
