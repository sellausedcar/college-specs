# Comparison Websites:

## 1. College Scorecard (https://collegescorecard.ed.gov/compare)

**Headline metrics** (pinned at the top of every comparison)
- Average Annual Cost
- Graduation Rate
- Median Earnings

**College Information**
- Full-time Enrollment (share enrolled full-time)
- Institution profile card — year, control (public / private nonprofit / for-profit), locale, size category, number of undergraduates, website
- Socio-Economic Diversity (% Pell recipients)
- Race/Ethnicity breakdown (selectable per group)

**Costs**
- Average Annual Cost (vs. national / college-type midpoint)
- Average Cost by Family Income Category ($0–30k, $30–48k, …)

**Graduation & Retention**
- Graduation Rate (with a "Pell recipients only" toggle)
- Students Who Return After Their First Year (retention)
- Outcomes 8 Years After Attending — graduated / withdrew / transferred / still enrolled (filterable by started-here vs. transferred-in, full- vs. part-time)

**Financial Aid & Debt**
- Students Receiving Federal Loans (by loan type)
- Median Total Debt After Graduation
- Typical Monthly Loan Payment (10-yr standard plan)
- Repayment Rate (% "making progress," etc., 2 years after entering repayment)

**Typical Earnings**
- Median Earnings (10 years after entering)
- Median Earnings Compared to High School Graduates (% of alumni out-earning a typical HS grad)

**Test Scores & Acceptance**
- Test Scores — SAT Reading range, SAT Math range, ACT range
- Acceptance Rate

## 2. College Navigator (https://nces.ed.gov/collegenavigator)

**Institution identity** (shown at the top of every comparison)
- City, State
- Distance from your ZIP code
- Type (2/4-year; public / private nonprofit / private for-profit)
- Campus setting (locale)

**Estimated Student Expenses (Before Aid)**
- Total cost of attendance by living arrangement — On Campus, Off Campus, Off Campus with Family
- Split by In-state vs. Out-of-state
- Multiyear Tuition Calculator (projects total tuition & fees using a chosen inflation rate)

**Financial Aid**
- Percent awarded any financial aid
- Grant aid — % awarded & average amount, broken out by Total grants, Federal, Pell, Other Federal, State/Local, Institutional
- Student loans — % awarded & average amount, broken out by Total, Federal, Non-federal

**Net Price**
- Average net price (students paying in-state tuition, awarded aid)
- Net price by family income bracket ($0–30k, $30–48k, $48–75k, $75–110k, $110k+)

**Enrollment**
- Total enrollment
- Undergraduate enrollment
- Undergraduate transfer-in enrollment
- Undergraduates by attendance status (full-time / part-time)
- Undergraduates by gender (male / female)
- Undergraduates by race / ethnicity
- Undergraduates by age (24 and under / 25 and over)
- Undergraduates by residence (in-state / out-of-state / foreign)
- Undergraduates by distance-education status (all online / some online / none)

**Admissions**
- Undergraduate application fee
- Percent admitted (acceptance rate)
- Percent admitted who enrolled (yield)
- SAT Evidence-Based Reading & Writing (25th / 50th / 75th percentile)
- SAT Math (25th / 50th / 75th percentile)
- ACT Composite (25th / 50th / 75th percentile)
- ACT English (25th / 50th / 75th percentile)
- ACT Math (25th / 50th / 75th percentile)

**Retention and Graduation Rates**
- Retention rate — full-time / part-time first-time students
- Percentage of entering students counted in the graduation rate
- Overall graduation rate
- Transfer-out rate
- Bachelor's degree completion rate at 4 / 5 / 6 years

**Outcome Measures**
- Award & enrollment status 8 years after entry — received a certificate / received a Bachelor's / still enrolled at same institution / enrolled at a different institution
- Broken down by entering status (first-time / non-first-time), attendance (full-time / part-time), and Pell status (Pell / non-Pell)

## 3. Common Data Set — CDS (https://commondataset.org)

*Not a comparison site but a standardized template every college self-publishes (usually via its Institutional Research office). Data is organized into 10 lettered sections, A–J, identical across all schools.*

**A. General Information**
- Institution name, address, and contact information
- Control / affiliation (public, private nonprofit, private for-profit; religious affiliation)
- Academic calendar (semester, quarter, trimester, etc.)
- Degrees / awards offered

**B. Enrollment and Persistence**
- Enrollment by racial/ethnic category and gender (degree-seeking vs. non-degree, undergraduate vs. graduate)
- Enrollment by attendance status (full-time / part-time)
- Number of degrees awarded
- Retention rate (first-time, first-year students)
- Graduation rates — 4-year / 5-year / 6-year, for first-time, full-time bachelor's-seeking students

**C. First-Time, First-Year (Freshman) Admission**
- Applications, admitted, and enrolled counts (by gender)
- Wait-list offered / accepted / admitted
- Acceptance rate and yield
- High school units required / recommended; GPA and class-rank requirements
- **Relative importance of each admission factor** — rigor of record, class rank, GPA, recommendations, test scores, application essay, interview, extracurriculars, talent/ability, character, first-generation, alumni relation, geographic residence, state residency, religious affiliation, volunteer & work experience, level of applicant's interest
- Standardized-test policy (required / recommended / considered; how used)
- SAT/ACT score distributions — EBRW, Math, ACT Composite & subscores (25th / 50th / 75th percentiles); % submitting each test
- High school GPA distribution / average GPA; class-rank distribution
- Early decision / early action; application fee and deadlines

**D. Transfer Admission**
- Transfer applications, admitted, and enrolled counts
- Application requirements, minimum credits, required items, and deadlines

**E. Academic Offerings and Policies**
- Special study options (accelerated program, co-op, distance learning, double major, dual enrollment, ESL, honors, independent study, internships, study abroad, teacher certification, ROTC, weekend college, etc.)
- General-education / core curriculum requirements

**F. Student Life**
- Percentages of undergraduates — from out of state, from other countries, living on campus, in fraternities / sororities
- Student activities and organizations
- Campus security services (24-hour patrol, late-night transport, etc.)
- Student services (health, counseling, career, etc.); housing options

**G. Annual Expenses**
- Tuition & required fees — by residency (in-district / in-state / out-of-state) and by program
- Room and board (on-campus)
- Books and supplies
- Other / personal expenses
- Comprehensive fee and estimated total cost of attendance

**H. Financial Aid**
- Scholarships / grants awarded — federal, state, institutional, external
- Self-help aid — loans and work-study
- Number / percent of students receiving need-based and non-need-based aid
- Average financial aid package; average need-based grant
- Average net price; percent of need met
- Average indebtedness of graduates
- Aid deadlines and required forms (FAFSA, CSS Profile)

**I. Instructional Faculty and Class Size**
- Number of instructional faculty (full-time / part-time / total)
- Percent with a terminal degree; percent female
- Student-to-faculty ratio
- Number of undergraduate class sections by size (2–9, 10–19, 20–29, 30–39, 40–49, 50–99, 100+)
- Class sub-sections (labs / discussions) by size

**J. Disciplinary Areas of Degrees Conferred**
- Percentage of undergraduate degrees awarded by CIP discipline area (e.g., engineering, biological sciences, business, social sciences, health professions)

## 4. CollegeSimply (https://www.collegesimply.com/colleges/compare)

*A commercial site comparing 50+ facts for 2,500+ colleges (up to four at once). Data sourced from NCES/IPEDS, College Scorecard, and Peterson's directory data.*

**Overview**
- Location
- Setting (locale)
- Enrollment
- Type (public / private, 4-year)
- National Ranking (CollegeSimply's own ranking)
- Religious Affiliation
- Status (non-profit / for-profit)
- Website
- Team Colors

**Costs**
- Tuition (in-state)
- Tuition (out-of-state)
- Room & Board
- Average Net Price
- Average Grant Aid
- % Receiving Grant Aid

**Admission**
- Applied / Admitted (counts)
- Acceptance Rate
- Enrolled % (yield)
- Average High School GPA
- Typical High School Grades (letter grade)
- Admissions Standards (rating label, e.g. "Elite")
- Applicant Competition (rating label, e.g. "Extreme")
- ACT Range (25th/75th)
- SAT Range (25th/75th)
- Submit Test Scores (test policy)
- Application Deadline

**Academics**
- Student-to-Faculty Ratio
- Popular Majors (top 5, with % of degrees)

**Outcomes**
- Graduation Rate
- Median Earnings (after 10 years)
- Making Student Loan Payments (% repaying)
- Median Student Loan Debt
- Median Loan Payment (monthly)

**Diversity**
- % Men / % Women
- Racial Diversity (rating label)
- HBCU (yes / no)
- Race/ethnicity breakdown — % White, Black, Hispanic, Native American, Pacific Islander, Two or More Races, International, Unknown

**Students**
- % In-State
- % Out-of-State

**Campus**
- Campus Housing (yes / no)
- Freshman Dorm Required
- Meal Plan
- Weekend / Evening Classes
- Daycare Facility

**Auto-generated "Comparable Facts"**
- Plain-English summary sentences derived from the factors above (cost, admissions difficulty, and graduation-outcome comparisons) — a presentation feature, not new data

## 5. Niche (https://www.niche.com/colleges/compare)

*A commercial site. The free compare tool is a single scrollable table (not grouped into sections); richer letter-grade "report cards" live on individual college profiles, behind sign-up, not in the compare view.*

**Comparison columns (in table order)**
- College — name, location, type (College)
- Your chances — "Will you get in?" admission-odds predictor (interactive)
- Full-time enrollment
- Acceptance rate
- Graduation rate
- Net price
- SAT range (25th/75th)
- ACT range (25th/75th)
- In-state tuition
- Out-of-state tuition
- Median earnings 5 years after graduation
- Employed 5 years after graduation (% employed)

**Direct Admissions columns** (Niche's guaranteed-offer program)
- Merit scholarship (offer amount, if any)
- Expiration date (of the offer)
- Next steps (whether the college participates in Direct Admissions)

**AI Comparison**
- A generated narrative paragraph summarizing the key differences (acceptance, earnings, graduation, cost) — a presentation feature, not new data
